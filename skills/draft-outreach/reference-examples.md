# Reference examples: the humanizer's voice anchor

These are short, hand-written, human-sounding outreach touches, one per register.
Phase 4 of `/draft-outreach` hands the humanizer the single example whose label
matches the current register, plus the assembled draft, and asks it to rewrite the
draft in this voice and rhythm.

These are anchors, not templates. The humanizer should match the cadence, opener
style, vulnerability, and sign-off, NOT copy facts or phrasing. Every fact must
come from the draft.

Style rules baked into these examples on purpose:
- No em dashes or en dashes. Hyphens with spaces, commas, or periods only.
- Short. One specific, verifiable hook. One ask, or none.
- At least one honest, slightly vulnerable line. This is what reads human.
- Plain words. No "delve", no "leverage", no "at the intersection of".

If you have your own better example for a register, replace the matching block
below (or point `voice.reference_example_path` in your config at your own file).
